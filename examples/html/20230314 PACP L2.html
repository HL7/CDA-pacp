Administrative Information

It is very important for you to discuss your medical treatment goals and wishes with your healthcare agent, your family, and your medical care providers. Keep in mind that advance medical directives are simply expressions of your medical treatment goals and preferences. There is no guarantee that your medical care providers will follow all of your wishes, but one thing is certain: If your advance medical directives cannot be quickly located and retrieved in a time of need, then medical care providers, your family and friends will not be able to take your wishes into consideration when they make critical decisions regarding your treatment.

I am emotionally and mentally competent to make this personal advance directive document. I understand the purpose and effect of this personal advance directive document, I agree with everything that is written in this personal advance directive document, and I have made this personal advance directive document knowingly, willingly and after careful deliberation.

If at some point in the future I am declared incompetent, I DO NOT want to be allowed to override these preferences. I want my doctors to follow the preferences I express in this document.

Healthcare Goals and Treatment Preferences

My Advance Care Goals

If I am so sick or seriously injured that I cannot express my own medical treatment preferences, and if I am not expected to live without additional treatment for my illness, disease, condition or injury, then I want my medical care team to know that these are the things that are most important to me:
  1. Avoiding prolonged dependence on machines
  2. Not being a physical burden to my family
  3. Dying at home

Treatment Preferences
If I am having significant pain or suffering, these are my thoughts about Supportive and Palliative Care. I would like my doctors to consult a Supportive and Palliative Care Team to help treat my physical, emotional and spiritual discomfort, and to support my family.

In addition to the general advance care goals provided above, below are specific treatment preferences with respect to certain specific circumstances or situations.

If my health ever deteriorates due to a terminal illness, and my doctors believe I will not be able to interact meaningfully with my family, friends, or surroundings, these are my thoughts about life-sustaining treatments. I would like for them to keep trying life-sustaining treatments until my healthcare agent decides it is time to stop and such treatments and let me die gently.

If I have a severe, irreversible brain injury or illness and can’t dress, feed, or bathe myself, or communicate my medical wishes, but doctors can keep me alive in this condition for a long period of time, these are my thoughts about life-sustaining treatments. I would like for them to keep trying life-sustaining treatments until my healthcare agent decides it is time to stop and such treatments and let me die gently.

Although I understand that, depending on the situation and circumstances, medical personnel may not be able to follow my wishes, here are my general thoughts on cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR):
I want my healthcare agent to decide for me.

Where to spend final days: At home.

Thought on hospice care: I would like to receive hospice care at home if possible.

I understand that, in certain jurisdictions, if I have been diagnosed as pregnant and that diagnosis is known to my attending physician, medical treatment providers may refuse to follow my directives and provide life-sustaining treatment including artificially administered nutrition and hydration, as well as CPR and other resuscitation measures.
